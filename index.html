Make sure to vote.

Elections in a democracy must be held regularly. They must be run according to fair rules. The German Basic Law states which rules apply. Two important rules: Everyone must be able to choose freely, without external coercion. Elections must be secret.

Why you should vote

5 reasons you should vote.

1. Elections have consequences.

You have the power to decide on the quality of life you want for yourself and future generations. Voting is your chance to stand up for the issues you care about like public transportation ect.. This is your life: take the time to help decide what’s best.

2. Not voting is giving up your voice.

Elections are decided by the people who go out and vote. Take some time and learn about the measures and the candidates. If you don’t vote, someone else will make the decision for you. Your power is in your vote.

3. It's your money.

You pay taxes, but do you know how that money is being used? Most people don’t. Voting is your chance to choose how your taxes are spent.

4. So history doesn’t have to repeat itself.

Generations before us fought hard to protect and expand our right to vote. Those fights shouldn’t have to be repeated. Using your vote is essential to protecting the voting rights victories we’ve already won, and to expanding those victories to ensure that every person who should be able to vote in Gemany is able to do so.

5.The winner of the election will represent you.

Voting for a candidate isn’t an endorsement, it’s a preference. It’s rare to agree with a candidate on every issue, but it’s important to make sure they know what issues you care about. There’s usually one candidate who represents your interests better than the others.

The three pillars of democracy.

 

E X E K U T I V E

This is the executive power. It must carry out the decisions of the legislature. Everyone knows the executive: the executive includes, for example, the police officer or the city administration. And if you look at the heads of our state: here the government is the executive branch. These include the Federal Chancellor and ministers. At the level of the federal states, the prime ministers and ministers of a state belong to it. The executive is responsible for ensuring that the laws are implemented in everyday life. She has to make sure everything goes right.

 

L E G I S L A T I V E

The legislature has the power to make laws. In Germany, the legislature includes the Bundestag, the Bundesrat and the state parliaments. There sit representatives who were elected by the people. They discuss possible laws, which are then binding for the other powers. The laws must first be signed by the Federal President before they can come into force.

 

J U D I K A T I V E

All courts belong to the judiciary. Different courts are responsible for different tasks. One decides how people are punished who have broken the law. Another decides whether a proposed law conforms to the requirements of the Constitution. You are independent and must not be influenced by anyone. However, they must of course make judgments that they can base on existing laws. So you can't just say, "You're going to jail because I think you're stupid." That's not an excuse.

How it works.

An electoral system is the rules by which an election is conducted. In the Bundestag elections, the electoral system determines how the members of the Bundestag are elected. These rules form the electoral system. Voters receive a ballot paper. Enter your choice there. Everyone has two votes, so they can put two crosses on the ballot paper. With the first vote, the voter decides which person from their constituency should come to the Bundestag as a member of parliament. A party is elected with the second vote. How many MPs a party later has in the Bundestag depends on the second votes.
